Lotus - Optimizing a Spark Ignition Engine

The Spark Ignition Engine Optimization project aims to improve engine efficiency and performance using advanced control strategies. The study focuses on Variable Valve Timing (VVT), Variable Valve Lift (VVL), and Variable Intake Runner Length, leveraging 1D engine simulations for optimization.

Key Optimizations:

  • Variable Valve Timing (VVT): Adjusts intake/exhaust valve timing for better combustion efficiency and fuel economy.
  • Variable Valve Lift (VVL): Modifies valve lift height to enhance airflow and power delivery across different RPM ranges.
  • Variable Intake Runner Length: Dynamically alters intake runner length to maximize volumetric efficiency at various engine speeds.

Results & Impact:

  • Up to 30% increase in power and torque at high RPMs.
  • Significant reduction in fuel consumption, leading to improved efficiency.
  • Better combustion control, reducing emissions and optimizing engine performance.
