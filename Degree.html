Driver’s Seat Suspension System

This project focuses on the design, modeling, and simulation of a driver's seat suspension system aimed at improving comfort and reducing whole-body vibrations.

The study begins with an analysis of the effects of vibrations on drivers and explores existing suspension solutions (passive, semi-active, and active). A mathematical model of a mass-spring-damper system is developed to simulate the behavior of the seat suspension.

Using SolidWorks, the project includes 3D modeling of a passive seat suspension system with an air spring and hydraulic damper, as well as an alternative design using tension springs. The structural performance of the system is then analyzed using the finite element method (FEM) in Ansys to evaluate its effectiveness in absorbing shocks and vibrations.
